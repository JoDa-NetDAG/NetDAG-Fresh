Community Trust-first Gas-free UX Provenance

What we are (in plain English).

NetDAG is a stability-first ecosystem designed to survive multiple market cycles. We earn trust through transparency and real-world utility: predictable liquidity via the bonding curve, measurable risk signals via Guardian, private connectivity via dVPN, and authenticity verification via Provenance. Easy Access (gas-free UX) ties it together so normal users can actually participate.

NetDAG values & business plan

The business goal is not “more hype”—it’s durable usage. Partners should be able to buy trust rails with simple pricing (per scan, per session, per API), while the protocol converts a portion of revenue into long-term support (buy-backs/reserve), subject to governance and safety.

Trust over marketing

Publish verifiable addresses and policies. Make system actions auditable. Never ask users to “just believe.”

Predictable liquidity

Bonding-curve + reserve shock absorber aims to reduce panic mechanics and increase usability for real activity.

Gas‑free Easy Access

Embedded wallets + optional sponsored transactions reduce onboarding failures and make utility flows complete.

Provenance that brands can use

QR/NFC verification, anti-counterfeit proofs, and audit trails—priced and packaged in a partner-friendly way.

Privacy as a utility

dVPN helps protect everyday users, while enabling node operators to earn for providing real network value.

Partner revenue flywheel

Partner usage → protocol revenue → buy-backs/reserve support → resilience → more partner trust.

Phase 0 · Foundations

Concept, math & security-first architecture

In motion

Define the rules, validate the economics, and prepare the system so every claim is verifiable and every contract is reviewable.

  • Formalize the pillars: Bonding Curve, Guardian, Provenance, dVPN, Easy Access.
  • Reserve/curve parameter tuning and documentation (including the 22% reserve shock absorber).
  • Security model: multi-sig treasury, timelocks, and audit readiness.
  • Partner packaging: simple pricing concepts + integration paths (scan/session/API).

Phase 1 · Launch

Core contracts + transparency dashboards

Upcoming

Launch the on-chain core with maximum clarity: verified addresses, readable policies, and public monitoring.

  • Deploy NDG token, reserve, and governance-safe admin controls.
  • Publish dashboards for reserve flows and system activity.
  • Guardian (beta) in advisory mode: risk signals, anomaly warnings, health metrics.

Phase 2 · Adoption

Easy Access (gas-free UX) becomes default

Planned

Make crypto feel normal: login flows, embedded wallets, recovery options, and (where enabled) sponsored gas so users see $0 fees.

  • Email/social onboarding + embedded wallet creation.
  • Optional paymaster sponsorship so users see $0 gas.
  • Batched actions (buy + stake, verify + mint proof, claim + reward).

Phase 3 · Utility

dVPN + Provenance in real-world pilots

Planned

Ship recurring-use products that create real demand: privacy sessions and authenticity scans—tracked, measurable, and partner-ready.

  • dVPN client + node stack with rewards tied to real network value.
  • Provenance dApp with QR/NFC verification and clear “AUTHENTIC” proofs.
  • SDKs and docs for integrators; analytics feed Guardian insights.

Phase 4 · Scale

Partnership revenue + conversion loops

Future

Make adoption easy for brands: simple pricing, predictable service, and a clear path from partner usage to protocol support.

  • Partner plans (per scan/session/API) + onboarding playbooks.
  • Governance-defined policy for converting a portion of revenue into NDG buy-backs/reserve support.
  • Loyalty/rewards programs tied to verifiable actions (not fake volume).

Phase 5 · Maturity

Guardian active defense + long-term governance

Long-term

Graduate from advisory signals to carefully limited, transparent safety actions—then progressively hand the keys to community governance.

  • Governance-tuned thresholds and transparent circuit-breaker protections.
  • Public reporting of every Guardian action and outcome.
  • Community-led upgrades, grants, and long-term funding for audits/public goods.

How to read this roadmap

Phases are sequenced, not calendar-locked. Some will overlap and some may shift due to audits, regulation, or partner feedback. The goal is to ship responsibly and report progress honestly.
