Why partner with NetDAG

Many Web3 integrations fail for one reason: they ask mainstream users to accept volatility, confusing UX, and unverifiable claims. NetDAG is designed to be the opposite—stable-by-design mechanics, measurable risk signals, and real-world products that partners can sell.

  • Predictable economics: bonding-curve dynamics + reserve “shock absorber” aim to smooth liquidity and reduce fragility.
  • Guardian intelligence: public risk metrics (concentration, anomalies, drain signals) that help partners operate responsibly.
  • Real-world utility: Provenance for authenticity + dVPN for privacy are designed to create recurring usage.
  • Easy Access UX: optional gas-free flows, embedded wallets, and onboarding patterns that feel familiar.
  • Transparency: publish addresses, dashboards, and policy changes to reduce integration risk.
  • Charity pledge (accurate): a public 2% giving pledge with reporting—not currently enforced as immutable contract logic.

How integration works (practical steps)

  1. Discovery: define goals, user journey, trust requirements, and success metrics.
  2. Technical plan: choose SDK/API paths, auth (Easy Access vs wallet-native), environments, and data needs.
  3. Pilot: limited rollout with measurement (conversion, fraud reduction, retention, support load).
  4. Scale: harden security, finalize pricing, expand geography/products, and publish case studies.
What we optimize for: fewer user drop-offs, fewer fraud events, clearer audit trails, and stable operational economics. If an integration cannot be measured, it usually cannot scale—so we instrument pilots from day one.
Security & compliance: sensitive integrations go through security review and governance sign-off for parameter changes. We prefer careful growth over rushed deployments.

Suggested pilot KPIs (ideas you can keep or remove)

  • Conversion: % of users who complete onboarding (Easy Access) and first action.
  • Fraud reduction: counterfeit claims reduced; verified authenticity rate.
  • Support load: tickets per 1,000 users (wallet issues, lost access, gas confusion).
  • Retention: repeat scans/sessions over 7/30/90 days.
  • Revenue: partner usage fees (per scan/session/API) + predictability.
  • Network health: dVPN node uptime, bandwidth served, geographic coverage.
  • Trust metrics: audit trail completeness, time-to-verify, dispute resolution time.
  • Governance readiness: clarity of policy changes and partner communications.
