NetDAG Roadmap

A stability-first path: ship foundations, prove utility, scale partnerships, and graduate to community governance— without chasing hype dates.

Trust & Transparency Bonding-curve stability Guardian AI Gas-free UX dVPN privacy Provenance

Values & Business Plan (what guides every phase)

NetDAG is designed as infrastructure—a stability-first crypto economy where real usage matters more than short-term price action. The roadmap below is sequenced around measurable outcomes: security, adoption, partner revenue, and long-term resilience.

Trust over hype

Proof beats claims: transparent addresses, clear token flows, auditable system behavior, and simple user safety rules.

Stability-first token design

Bonding-curve + reserve “shock absorber” aims to reduce fragility and build predictable liquidity for real users.

Gas-free Easy Access

Crypto must feel normal: onboarding that looks like Web2, embedded wallets, and optional sponsored gas for users.

Real-world commerce

Provenance is built for brands: QR/NFC verification, anti-counterfeit protection, and audit trails that outlive labels.

Privacy as a utility

dVPN turns bandwidth into a privacy network—so everyday users can connect without trusting a single central provider.

Partner-friendly revenue model

Make adoption easy: partners can pay simple fees (per scan/session/API), while the protocol converts a portion into NDG buy-backs.

Business Flywheel Utility → partner revenue → buy-backs → resilience → more utility

Phase 0 · Foundations

Concept, math & architecture

Status: In motion

Build the stability-first core: define rules, publish docs, and prepare contracts and dashboards so every claim can be verified.

  • Define the mission: stability-first, real-world-linked crypto economy.
  • Formalize pillars: Financial Engine, Guardian/Neuron, Provenance, plus Easy Access UX.
  • Design curve parameters and the 22% reserve shock absorber with clear, explainable mechanics.
  • Prepare partner-facing docs: fee model, onboarding plan, and proof flows (QR/NFC, scan verification).
  • Architecture for Guardian signals, oracle feeds, and dashboards.
  • Security planning: contract boundaries, multi-sig custody, and audit readiness.

Phase 1 · Launch

Presale, core contracts & Guardian (beta)

Status: Upcoming

Launch the on-chain core with maximum transparency: verified addresses, readable policies, and a Guardian layer that measures risk in public.

  • Deploy NDG token, bonding-curve reserve, and timelock/treasury contracts.
  • Publish verifiable addresses and dashboards for reserve/flows.
  • Presale: transparent curve steps, supply caps, and clear user guidance.
  • Guardian beta in advisory mode: concentration alerts, liquidity signals, anomaly detection.
  • Enable the first “utility rails”: initial buy, stake (if live), and ecosystem onboarding.

Phase 2 · User adoption

Easy Access (gas-free UX) + first utility loops

Status: Planned

Make the experience feel normal. Adoption is a UX problem as much as a tech problem—and gas-free flows remove the biggest failure points.

  • Release Easy Access onboarding: email/social sign-in, embedded wallet creation, recovery options.
  • Optional sponsored transactions so users see $0 gas when enabled.
  • Batch actions: buy + stake, verify + mint proof, claim + reward.
  • Education & safety prompts: “verify addresses”, “avoid fake links”, “readable transaction previews”.

Phase 3 · Network utilities

dVPN + Provenance / RWA dApp

Status: Planned

Ship utilities that create real, recurring demand: privacy connectivity and verifiable authenticity for physical goods.

  • dVPN client + node stack with NDG-denominated bandwidth and rewards.
  • Provenance dApp for verifying products using QR/NFC, with clear “AUTHENTIC” proofs.
  • SDKs and docs for third parties (brands, dev teams, integrators).
  • Usage metrics feed Guardian analytics: scans, sessions, node health, fraud signals.
  • Incentives based on verifiable activity, not fake volume.

Phase 4 · Commerce & partnerships

Brand rails, pricing, and revenue conversion

Status: Future

Scale through business reality: partners want simple pricing, reliability, and measurable value. NetDAG aims to make “trust rails” easy to buy.

  • Partner plans: per-scan, per-session, per-API usage pricing (minimizes token risk for brands).
  • Revenue policy: convert a portion of partner revenue into NDG buy-backs on the curve (subject to governance).
  • Loyalty programs tied to verified actions (scan/verify/connect), not speculation.
  • Case studies + industry templates (luxury, pharma, electronics, tickets).

Phase 5 · Guardian upgrade

From advisory to limited active defense

Status: Vision

Upgrade Guardian carefully. Any “active” behavior must be limited, transparent, and governed—because safety without accountability becomes risk.

  • Governance-tuned risk thresholds and intervention rules.
  • On-chain “circuit-breaker” style protections for extreme events (with public reporting).
  • Transparency dashboards for every Guardian action and outcome.
  • Opt-in profiles (conservative/neutral/advanced) to respect different user preferences.

Phase 6 · Governance & autonomy

NetDAG as self-sustaining infrastructure

Status: Long-term

Mature into a multi-cycle protocol: community-led upgrades, sustainable treasury, and independent builders extending NetDAG.

  • Progressively hand key parameters to on-chain governance.
  • Grow independent teams building specialized apps on NetDAG rails.
  • Multi-chain expansion where it adds resilience and user access.
  • Long-term funding for audits, public goods, and ecosystem maintenance.

How to read this roadmap

Phases are sequenced, not calendar-locked. Some will overlap, and some may be reordered by audits, regulation, or partner feedback. The goal is to ship responsibly and report progress honestly.

Guiding principles

Every step should respect these guardrails:

Stability before speculation On-chain transparency Security over speed Real usage > fake volume Partner-friendly pricing Community alignment
Timeline at a glance Foundations → Utility → Partnerships → Governance
NetDAG is deliberately patient: the goal is to build infrastructure that survives multiple cycles. If you are here only for a quick flip, this roadmap will feel too slow—and that is by design.

Roadmaps are living documents. As audits complete and pilot partnerships go live, this page will be updated with verified links, metrics, and progress reports.
