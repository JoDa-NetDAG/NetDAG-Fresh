Friction is the real enemy

Traditional crypto is unnecessarily complicated. Wallet downloads, seed phrases, and gas fees create a maze before a user can do anything useful. That’s why adoption stalls: it’s not that people hate crypto — they hate friction.

NetDAG’s “Easy Access” experience is designed to remove those blockers. You log in with an email or a familiar provider like Google, your wallet is created automatically in the background, and transactions can be sponsored so you don’t see gas.

The goal is simple: make your first on-chain action feel as natural as your first click in any app.

What you get (in plain English)

  • No Wallet Required — Sign Up Like Email: Log in with email or social accounts. An embedded wallet is created behind the scenes. You can still view your address and balance — but you’re not forced to “be a crypto expert” to start.
  • No Seed Phrases — Recovery That Matches Real Life: Instead of handing new users a 12–24 word phrase and calling it “security,” modern embedded wallets can support account recovery via email/SMS/device-based verification (depending on the chosen setup). This dramatically reduces the most common failure mode in crypto: lost access.
  • Zero Gas Fees (for the user) — Gas Sponsorship: The user can see $0.00 gas at checkout because a paymaster can sponsor fees in the background. You click “Confirm” and move on — no need to buy BNB just to pay network costs.

So what powers this?

NetDAG’s Easy Access is built with modern Web3 infrastructure patterns — the same direction the industry is moving as a whole. For authentication, services like Google enable “Continue with Google” onboarding.

For wallet creation, embedded wallet providers (examples include Privy and Magic) can generate wallets inside the app experience. Users can interact immediately, while advanced users can still choose more traditional setups when desired.

For gasless transactions, Account Abstraction (ERC‑4337) enables smart accounts that can submit operations through a bundler. A paymaster can then sponsor gas so users don’t need to own BNB just to move tokens. Infrastructure platforms like Biconomy provide tooling in this category (smart accounts, bundlers, paymasters).

Finally, platforms that focus on complete onboarding flows — wallet + login + gas sponsorship patterns — can simplify integration. A good example category is Openfort, which emphasizes smooth “gasless transactions” UX patterns for consumer apps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is this really free forever?

A: The goal is sustainable, long‑term gas sponsorship where enabled. When gasless mode is active, users won’t need to hold a gas token just to interact. Sponsorship can be managed via paymasters and dedicated budgets.

Q: Can NetDAG access my funds?

A: No. Your wallet is designed to be non-custodial — only you can authorize transactions. NetDAG can sponsor gas fees, but sponsoring gas is not the same thing as controlling funds.

Q: What if I want to use MetaMask instead?

A: You can. Easy Access is about onboarding. Power users can still connect traditional wallets and interact normally.

Q: How is this safer than traditional wallets?

A: Traditional users lose access when seed phrases are lost. Embedded wallet flows can support modern recovery methods (email / device / trusted recovery), while smart wallets can add advanced security controls.

Q: What happens if one provider changes or shuts down?

A: The goal is portability and resilience. Because the wallet is on-chain and user-authorized, the experience can evolve: if one infrastructure option changes, users should still be able to keep using NDG via standard wallet routes.
